Project Demand Forecaster

Our goal is automatic forecasting of demand.

The aim of that project is applying modern scientific methods for forecasting of demand. If you are owner of a small store you are always worried about goods order. Sometimes you order too much or too little goods it leads to loosing money. We want to help you by providing forecasting of demand for your goods.You just have to prepare a xls document and use our service.Everything is absolutely free. We are always open for comment and criticism that is why all source codes are open and available by GitHub.

How does it work

1.) You create a xls file by template. It contains some statistical information needed for building prediction.

2.) You use Start forecasting for sending that xls file.

3.) We are trying to make a forecast by your data.

4.) We send you an Email message with result.

5.) Your data is automatically deleted from our server.

How to create xls file

The xls file with data has to have only four columns for demand forecasting.If you want to calculate elasticity you have to add column with prices.

Format of xls file
Column name Description Example
whs_id It's just cooked-up code of your store. That column is invented for separation two or more stores in one xls file. Only digits are allowed. Example: 1 or 1234 or 2354
art_id It's just cooked-up code of your product. That column is invented for separation two or more products in one xls file. Only digits are allowed. Example: 1 or 1234 or 2354
day_id The date of measure. It has strict format: dd.mm.yyyy Example: 15.01.2017 or 17.05.2016
sale_qnty It tells how much the product you sold in the shop in that day. Example:17 or 184,5 or 54,0 or 25
price !!!Only for calculation of elasticity!!! It tells how much one unit of product costs. Example:17 or 184,5 or 54,0 or 25

How xls file for demand forecasting should look like

You can download example of xls or blank template .

MyDataForecast.xls
whs_id art_id day_id sale_qnty

1 12 16.12.2016 15,5
1 12 17.12.2016 16,0
1 12 18.12.2016 12,8
